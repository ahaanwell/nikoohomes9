Understanding RERA and Its Importance

The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act (RERA) was introduced to improve transparency, accountability, and buyer protection in India's real estate sector. Projects registered under RERA are required to comply with regulatory guidelines related to approvals, construction progress, financial management, and project disclosures.

For homebuyers, RERA registration provides access to verified project information, including approved plans, construction updates, possession timelines, and other statutory details. It also encourages greater transparency between developers and buyers throughout the project lifecycle.

Under Karnataka RERA regulations, developers are required to maintain project-specific financial records and follow prescribed compliance requirements. These measures are intended to improve project accountability and protect the interests of homebuyers.

Why RERA Registration Matters

Projects registered under Karnataka RERA are expected to comply with several regulatory requirements, including:

  • Registration with the Karnataka Real Estate Regulatory Authority.
  • Periodic construction progress updates.
  • Disclosure of approved project information.
  • Compliance with applicable legal approvals.
  • Financial transparency as prescribed under RERA.
  • Buyer protection through regulatory oversight.

Many banks and housing finance institutions also prefer projects with completed statutory approvals and valid RERA registration when processing home loan applications, subject to their internal policies.
